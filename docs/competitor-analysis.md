## Detailed Competitor Profiles

### 1. Foligain (foligain.com)

**What they sell:** Topical hair loss treatments combining FDA-approved minoxidil (2-5%) with their proprietary Trioxidil blend. Products for both men and women. Operating since 2008.

**SEO Keywords / Positioning:**
- Primary: "minoxidil for hair loss", "trioxidil hair treatment"
- Claims: 180% increase in visible hair volume at 4 months (cosmetic study)
- Position: science-backed topical treatments at mid-range pricing

**Structured Data:** Basic Shopify e-commerce schema (Product, JSON-LD). No advanced schema types (no FAQ, Article, HowTo, or review aggregate markup beyond default Shopify).

**llms.txt:** Not present at either /llms.txt or /.well-known/llms.txt

**Content Strategy:**
- Weak. Blog exists ("Foligain News") but appears product-focused with minimal educational content
- Separate storefronts for US, UK, and EU markets (foligain.com, foligain.co.uk, eu.foligain.com)
- Heavy reliance on Judge.me review widget for social proof
- No visible educational hub, guides, or comparison content

**SEO Strengths:**
- Long-established brand (since 2008)
- Proprietary ingredient (Trioxidil) creates unique keyword ownership
- Multiple regional domains for geo-targeting

**SEO Weaknesses:**
- Very thin content strategy — no educational blogging
- No advanced structured data
- No llms.txt or AI discovery strategy
- English-only within each storefront
- Regional domains split link equity instead of using hreflang on one domain

## Key Takeaways for Hairgenetix

### Where Hairgenetix Already Wins

1. **AI Discovery:** Hairgenetix is the ONLY competitor with llms.txt deployed. This is a significant first-mover advantage in AI-powered search (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Claude). None of the 5 competitors have implemented this.

2. **Schema Markup Diversity:** Hairgenetix has 10/10 schema diversity (FAQPage, Article, HowTo, BreadcrumbList) -- far ahead of all competitors who rely on basic Shopify/WordPress defaults.

3. **Multilingual:** Hairgenetix supports 9 languages. Every single competitor is English-only. This is a massive competitive moat for European and Scandinavian markets.

4. **Unique Product Category:** GHK-Cu copper peptide + mesotherapy device is a genuinely different approach. No competitor offers this combination. Foligain does minoxidil, Hairmax does lasers, Nutrafol does supplements, Keeps does prescriptions, MinoxidilMax does high-strength minoxidil. Hairgenetix occupies its own lane.

5. **Review Volume:** 1,200+ reviews at 4.6 stars is competitive. Only Nutrafol (doctor-backed) likely has more verified social proof.

### Where Hairgenetix Must Improve

1. **Content Strategy (CRITICAL):** This is the biggest gap. Hairgenetix has 3 content pages. Compare this to:
   - MinoxidilMax: 100+ articles across 22 categories
   - Nutrafol: extensive blog with 5+ categories and newsletter
   - Keeps: full educational hub with 4 categories, quizzes, expert interviews
   - **Recommendation:** Build a content hub with 20-30 articles covering copper peptide science, mesotherapy education, hair loss causes, comparison guides, and before/after case studies. Target informational keywords like "what is GHK-Cu for hair", "mesotherapy for hair loss", "copper peptide vs minoxidil".

2. **Domain Authority / Backlink Profile:** Nutrafol has DA 71 and 70K backlinks. Hairgenetix is likely DA 10-20. Building authority through:
   - Guest posts on hair loss and dermatology sites
   - PR mentions (press page is a good start)
   - Scientific citations and clinical study promotion
   - Influencer partnerships in European markets

3. **Technical SEO (83/100):** The 17-point gap is mainly H1 tags (theme limitation). Fixing this would push the score to 90+. Page speed should also be audited.

4. **Content Score (64/100):** The weakest dimension. Fix by:
   - Expanding thin product descriptions
   - Adding comparison guides (GHK-Cu vs minoxidil, vs finasteride, vs laser)
   - Creating ingredient education pages
   - Building a proper blog with regular publishing cadence
